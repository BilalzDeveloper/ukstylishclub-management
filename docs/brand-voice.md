# UKSC brand voice

> **Status: draft.** Written from the store's positioning (affordable branded
> menswear/streetwear, UK, 18–35, mobile-first). Owner: read the examples,
> correct anything that sounds wrong, and this file becomes the reference every
> skill writes with.

## Voice in one line

A mate with great taste who knows where the deals are — confident, direct,
never salesy-desperate, never trying too hard.

## Tone attributes

- **Confident, not hyped.** State why the piece is good; don't shout.
- **Direct and short.** Short sentences. One idea each. Mobile screens.
- **British English.** Colour, not color. Trainers, not sneakers (except
  hashtags where both help discovery).
- **Streetwear energy, light touch.** Current, casual — but readable by a
  35-year-old, not just an 18-year-old. No slang pile-ups.
- **Honest.** Never fake scarcity ("only 2 left!!" when untrue), never fake
  urgency, no invented reviews. Real drops, real prices, real stock.
- **Price-proud.** Affordability is a feature — say the price plainly and let
  it do the work.
- **Emoji-light.** Zero to two per social caption; none in product descriptions.

## Product descriptions

Structure (3 short paragraphs max):
1. What it is and the one reason it's worth it.
2. Fit/material/details that matter for buying (fit, fabric, sizing note).
3. Price line + sizes available.

**Before** (how products come out of onboarding today):
> Siim Nike T-shirt Classic

**After:**
> Nike cotton tee in washed black. Regular fit, heavyweight fabric that holds
> its shape — the kind you reach for three times a week.
>
> True to size. S–XXL.
>
> £24.99.

## Do / don't words

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| clean, solid, heavyweight, true to size, everyday, drop, restock | vibes, fire 🔥 (as a word), insane, must-have, obsessed, elevate, curated |
| "£24.99, sizes S–XXL" | "grab yours before it's gone!!!" |
| "New drop just landed" | "We are pleased to announce" |

## Social captions

- Lead with the product or the price, not with "Hey guys".
- One caption = one product or one clear theme.
- CTA is quiet: "Link in bio", "Sizes S–XXL on the site".
- Hashtags: 5–10, mix of broad + niche, e.g. `#menswearuk #ukstreetwear
  #mensfashionuk #trainersuk #ootdmen` + product-specific (`#nikeairmax`).

## Email

- Subject lines: plain and specific beats clever — "New drop: Nike tees from
  £24.99" outperforms "You won't believe what just landed 👀".
- One topic per email, one button, price visible before the click.
- Sign-off: "— UKSC".
